How To Optimize Your YouTube Videos For SEO
| By Nisar Hussain Laghari | Published on March 8, 2025

YouTube is the second-largest search engine after Google, with millions of videos competing for attention. In fact, eight out of ten video search results come from YouTube, and over 500 hours of video are uploaded every minute.

With so much content, how can you make sure your videos don’t get lost? The key is to optimize your videos for search using YouTube SEO best practices. By following the right strategies, you can increase visibility, reach more youtube viewers, and grow your channel.

Select a great keyword

To improve your YouTube SEO, you need to select the right keywords or phrases for your video. These keywords appear in titles, descriptions, and metadata, and if spoken in the video, they also show up in captions and transcripts making them even more valuable for search rankings.

Your keywords should:

  • Accurately describe your video content.
  • Match what users search for when looking for videos like yours.

To find the best keywords, you’ll need to do some keyword research. A simple way to start is by using YouTube’s Search Suggest. Type a relevant word or phrase in the search bar, and YouTube will show popular search suggestions. These are real terms people are using, making them great keyword choices for your video.

Include accurate captions

YouTube automatically generates captions for videos, but these auto-captions are only about 70% accurate, often leading to confusing or incorrect text.

Google prioritizes high-quality content and penalizes anything that seems like spam. Since poorly generated captions can look like nonsense, they might hurt your video’s search ranking.

To improve your YouTube SEO, it’s best to upload accurate closed captions using an SRT file. Search engines can’t watch videos, but they can read text. Well-written captions include relevant keywords, helping Google and YouTube understand your content and rank it properly.

Add a transcript 

Your video description is a great place to add a transcript on YouTube. The description box allows 5,000 characters, which is usually enough for a 10-minute video with a lot of dialogue.

If your transcript is too long or you want to include other important details—like a call-to-action—you can add a shorter version and link to the full transcript on another webpage.

Transcripts can also help boost SEO by turning your video content into useful, shareable material. You can repurpose transcripts for:

  • Blog posts
  • Infographics
  • Whitepapers
  • Fact sheets
  • Website content

Using transcripts in multiple formats makes your content more accessible and helps reach a wider audience.

Use a compelling thumbnail

Your video thumbnail plays a big role in getting people to click on your video. A high-quality, high-contrast image with a 16:9 aspect ratio works best. Close-up facial shots tend to get more attention.

How to Select or Create a Thumbnail

YouTube allows you to:

  • Choose from three auto-generated screenshots from your video.
  • Pick a different frame from your video.
  • Upload a custom thumbnail for a more professional look.

If you need a custom design, tools like Canva offer ready-made YouTube thumbnail templates.

Things to Keep in Mind

  • YouTube adds the video duration in the lower corner of the thumbnail, so avoid placing important elements there.
  • Google automatically filters out certain images, such as thumbnails containing the YouTube logo.
  • Your thumbnail should visually represent your target keyword, helping it appear in more relevant search results.

A well-designed thumbnail grabs attention, improves your click-through rate, and increases your video’s visibility.
